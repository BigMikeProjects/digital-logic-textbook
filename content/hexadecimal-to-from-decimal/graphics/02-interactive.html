Hexadecimal at a glance
Base 16 — a compact shorthand for binary. Quick recap before we convert.

Sixteen symbols

We count 0–9, then borrow letters A–F for the values 10–15 — because one digit must name sixteen different amounts.

One digit = one nibble

Since 16 = 2⁴, every hex digit packs exactly 4 bits. A byte is just two hex digits — that's why hex is really compressed binary.

Read a hex digit three ways at once: the symbol, its 4-bit pattern, and its decimal value. The rest of this video moves between those three.
