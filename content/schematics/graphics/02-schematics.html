Reading a Schematic

A schematic is a contract about intent
The same circuit can be drawn to confuse — or to communicate. Conventions exist to remove doubt.
Press M to toggle a messy draft against a clear one — same circuit, different intent.
Fan-out — one output driving several inputs (here G1 feeds both G2 and G3). Fan-in — the number of inputs into one gate (here G1 has 2).
Crossing ≠ connection
Wires that cross are separate nets — unless a junction dot says otherwise.
Gates carry direction: inputs → output
Distinctive shapes, signal flowing left to right. A bubble on a terminal means logical negation.
Use labels to tame long wires
Two stubs sharing a name are the same net — no wire need be drawn between them.
Standards evolve — adhere to clarity, not dogma
Documentation conventions adapt to workflows, so expect variance by era, region, and application.
Press R to swap the gate between the distinctive and rectangular symbol sets.
