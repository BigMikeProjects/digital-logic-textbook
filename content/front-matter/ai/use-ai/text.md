Artificial intelligence tools have become a normal part of how people learn, and in this course you are encouraged to use them. Used well, a generative AI assistant can act like a patient tutor that is available whenever you sit down to study — explaining a confusing idea a second or third way, generating extra practice problems, or quizzing you until a concept finally clicks.

If you have not tried it, a tool like NotebookLM is especially well suited to drill-and-practice. You can load it with the course readings and your own notes, then ask it to test you: generate flashcards, pose short-answer questions, or walk through a worked example one step at a time. This kind of active, repeated retrieval is one of the most effective ways to move material into long-term memory, and an AI assistant makes it easy to do as much of it as you need.

The reason to lean into AI for *practice* becomes clear when you look at how the course is graded. Exams are the largest part of your grade, and they are closed-notes and taken on paper. During an exam there is no device, no assistant, and no lookup — only what you carry in your own head. Everything you do between now and then should be aimed at that: building durable understanding you can reproduce and apply on your own.

That same framing explains where AI can quietly work against you. The smaller, low-stakes assignments — especially the reading quizzes — exist to make you engage with the material before class. If you hand those off to a chatbot to answer for you, you will collect the points, but you will have skipped the exact thinking the quiz was designed to produce. The cost is invisible at first and then very visible on an exam, where that missing practice becomes the difference between recognizing a problem and being stuck on it.

So the guideline for this course is simple: use AI to help you learn, not to avoid learning. Ask it to explain, to quiz you, to generate practice, and to check your reasoning. Don't ask it to do the work whose whole purpose was to build the skills you will be tested on. If a use of AI leaves you more capable when you close the laptop, it is helping. If it leaves you with a finished assignment but no new understanding, it is a detriment — and the exams will make that plain.
