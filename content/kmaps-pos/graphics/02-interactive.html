POS Minimization via K-Map
For Product of Sums (POS) form, we minimize F′ using the K-map — circling zeros exactly as SOP circles ones. Each group of zeros yields a product term in F′. DeMorgan's theorem then converts F′ → F as a product of sum terms.
Derivation
Step 1
F′ = A′B′ + B′D′
Read groups from K-map
Step 2
(F′)′ = (A′B′ + B′D′)′
Complement both sides
Step 3
F = (A′B′ + B′D′)′
(F′)′ = F
Step 4
F = (A′B′)′ · (B′D′)′
(P+Q)′ = P′·Q′
Step 5
F = (A+B) · (B+D)
(P·Q)′ = P′+Q′ per term
Result
F = (A+B)(B+D)
