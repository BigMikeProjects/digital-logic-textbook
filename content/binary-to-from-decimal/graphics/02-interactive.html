Why do computers count in binary?
Hardware is built from switches — and a switch only has two states.
🔌

Two states are easy to build

A transistor acts like a switch: OFF (≈0 V) or ON (≈ supply V). Two states are cheap, fast, and rock-solid to manufacture by the billion.

🛡️

Two states resist noise

Binary (2 levels)
1 · HIGH
forbidden
0 · LOW
Wide margins → noise ignored
Decimal (10 levels)
Cramped → a little noise flips the digit
🔢

Everything becomes bits

Numbers, text, images, audio — all encoded as 1s and 0s. Each bit answers one yes/no question, and n bits represent 2ⁿ distinct values.

One bit = one binary digit. To use these machines we constantly translate between the decimal we think in and the binary the hardware runs on — that's what the rest of this video covers.
